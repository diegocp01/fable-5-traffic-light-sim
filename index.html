How it works · rules
  • Arrivals: cars enter as a Poisson process — random exponential gaps, averaging the rate you set. Each driver gets their own desired speed and aggressiveness.
  • Car following: the Intelligent Driver Model (IDM) — drivers keep a speed-dependent safe gap and brake smoothly behind slower cars. Watch the stop-and-go wave ripple back when the light changes.
  • ● Green: proceed at desired speed.
  • ● Yellow: stop only if braking would be comfortable (≤ ~3.5 m/s²). Cars in the dilemma zone commit and clear the line instead.
  • ● Red: stop at the line; brake lights stay lit while held.
  • Pedestrians cross only during red, once the crosswalk is clear.
